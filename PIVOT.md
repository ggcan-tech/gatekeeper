Status: SIGNED — the company's written decision, within the 48h protocol window.
Verdict context: 2026-07-16 exam, LOSS on the named-judge +10 claim; both

## Decision

The company splits into a SHOP and a LAB, effective immediately.

### The Shop (revenue now): the Pre-Filing Bench Report

Product: a litigator sends their draft motion or brief. Within 24 hours they
receive (1) a bench-calibrated critique — how a judge of this court and
profile will attack the draft, its weak points, and what to fix; (2) an
outcome read with our PUBLISHED confidence framing (the replica's measured
+7-point edge over generic AI, labeled as exactly that — never sold as "your
judge's digital twin"); (3) an optional live rehearsal session against the
bench replica. Delivered concierge-style first (founders in the loop),
software later. Pilot price anchor: $2,500-$7,500 per motion, against the
$10-60K/weeks human mock-exercise budget line.

Method (the kitchen): "Monte Carlo moot" — internally, the argument is
simulated repeatedly (customer's position, opposing counsel, the bench
replica), dozens of runs with variations; the report distills which arguments
survive, which die, and the outcome distribution. The multi-party simulation
is an INTERNAL insight generator, never itself a validated claim; every
customer-facing number is anchored to the validated prediction layer.
Pitch line: "we argued your motion 50 times before you argue it once."

Claim discipline: "court-calibrated bench, validated methodology, measured
judge-specific edge of +7 points" — the named-judge margin is a disclosed
measurement, not a marketing promise. This is the only offer in the category
whose accuracy claims are pre-registered and public.

### The Lab (the claim we still want): Experiment #2

Target: push the judge-specific margin from +7 toward the frozen +10
product-grade bar. The ceiling is an OPEN EMPIRICAL QUESTION — the 81-82%
survey test-retest figure does not bind professional deciders constrained by
law and appellate review; lab task #0 is estimating judge self-consistency
from our own corpus (same judge, near-identical motions). Levers, in
priority order:
full opinion texts as grounding (v1 used docket descriptions only), PACER
briefs as inputs, per-motion-type personas, persona+retrieval ensemble,
deeper RSI loops. Infrastructure: the pipeline moves into the Archon
workflow engine — every lever a stage, every threshold a parameter, every
run tracked. Rules unchanged: iterate freely on pre-cutoff data; claim
improvements ONLY via a new public pre-registration sealed against a fresh
post-cutoff window (target: September 2026 rulings). No retakes on the
July window, ever.

### Explicitly deferred

- Live oral-argument simulation (v2): requires hearing-transcript ingestion
  and its own validation before any sale; without it, it is the cosplay this
  company exists to expose.
- Non-judge gatekeepers (regulators, procurement): the Series A expansion,
  earned wedge by wedge with the same sealed-experiment discipline.

### Priority rule through 2026-07-27 (YC deadline)

The shop owns the calendar: customer interviews, concierge pilots, and the
YC application outrank lab work. The lab gets remaining capacity. This
ordering is a hedge against the founder pattern on record since 2026-07-11
(build-comfort over talking to strangers) and is part of this decision.

## Why not stop

The exam produced: a working 78.7% engine near the physical ceiling, the
only public pre-registered validation dataset in the category, proof that
competitors' backward-looking accuracy claims are largely memorization
(name-only arm: 71.5%), and a buyer population already paying $10-60K for
unvalidated versions of the same rehearsal. Stopping now would abandon the
category's only credibility asset at the moment it was created.
