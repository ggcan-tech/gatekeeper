# PRODUCT MODEL — decided 2026-07-16 (senior-team research, 14 agents, 8 claims verified)

## The decision

**Primary: Model A — per-motion concierge Bench Report, $2,500-$7,500.**
Sequence: A now → C (litigation-funder underwriting screens) as a parallel
experiment at ≤15% founder time → B (firm subscription, ~N reports/mo,
$90-150K ACV) only when repeat A-buyers ask for committed pricing.

Why A (the YC-grade reasoning): it is the only model that can put paid,
repeat-purchase evidence in the bank before July 27; COGS is inference, so
"concierge" describes delivery, not economics; and the category's own history
rules: subscriptions without a workflow moment died (Gavelytics, June 2022),
per-litigation-event pricing scaled (Casetext $650M exit; EvenUp $2B+ at
~$300/demand; Darrow cash-positive on success fees). Revenue attaches to the
motion deadline, not the seat.

## 30-day proof test (this line goes in the YC application)

5 paid reports across 3 firms with ≥1 repeat order, plus 2 funder backtests
in flight. Q6 of the interview script (standing-order question) is the
trigger for offering Model B; funder Q4 answers are the go/no-go on C.

## Position + price anchors (verified)

- The artifact gap: every rival sells a session, a dashboard, or a scorecard
  of the LAWYER. **Nobody reads the actual draft and rehearses it.** We sell
  a document about their document.
- Price anchors: internal moot ≈ $15-40K of partner time (rates $2,100-3,400/hr);
  mock exercises $10-60K; consultant WRITTEN reports already sell at $7-12K;
  Pre/Dicta charges $1,800/case WITHOUT reading the complaint. Our $2.5-7.5K
  = 1-3% of a motion's cost.
- Named foils: Pre/Dicta (predicts from a docket number, never reads the
  brief; 85% claim is backtested, never post-cutoff) and Courtroom.ai
  ("over 90%," white-paper-on-request, invitation-only). Our counter is the
  standing offer: run any competitor's claim through our public post-cutoff
  protocol.
- The timing wedge: the SDNY pre-motion-letter stage and the
  substantially-complete-draft moment (5-10 days pre-filing). "A moot court
  for the brief" — because ~76% of these motions are decided on the papers.

## Copy / avoid (from the teardowns)

COPY: zero-effort input (email the draft, like Pre/Dicta's docket-number-in);
per-matter pricing; Bench IQ's narrow-wedge concierge launch and
"explanatory, not descriptive" positioning (ours: **"rehearsal, not
statistics"**); attach a named early-customer win when one exists.
AVOID: unverifiable accuracy claims; dashboards that make the lawyer do the
interpretation; self-serve billing dark patterns (Trellis: 195 unanswered BBB
complaints); avatar theater / live voice sparring (First Chair waitlist,
MootQourt invisible traction — crowded and unmonetized).

## Lab verdict (the honest 78→85 answer, pre-registered stop rule)

Absolute accuracy 83-86% is plausible; **the +10 judge-edge is likely
unreachable** because every good lever raises the name-only control too.
Sequence: ensemble voting (this week, +1-3 pts) → full PACER briefs
(2-3 weeks, +3-6 pts — gains from restoring case posture, not brief nuance) →
model refresh at next exam window (re-run name-only control every time) →
founder's inference-time research agent (+2-4 pts) ONLY with the time-boxed
contamination protocol (snapshot cut at fully-briefed date, docket canaries,
boxed-vs-unboxed audit). Skip: transcript mining, specialist personas.
**Always report TWO numbers** — absolute and judge-edge — conflating them is
how competitors minted fake 86.7% claims.
**Stop rule (pre-registered now):** if after briefs + ensemble + one model
upgrade the judge-edge's 95% CI upper bound is <+10 on ≥250 fresh post-cutoff
rulings in two consecutive exam windows, the digital-twin bar is declared
unreachable and the lab stands down that claim permanently.
