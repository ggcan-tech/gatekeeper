Defendants move to dismiss the Complaint in its entirety under Federal Rule of
Civil Procedure 12(b)(6) for failure to state a claim. Even accepting the
well-pleaded factual allegations as true, the Complaint offers only threadbare
recitals of the elements of a breach-of-contract claim and conclusory assertions
of harm. Plaintiff never identifies the specific contractual provision breached,
pleads no facts showing that any condition precedent was satisfied, and alleges
damages only in the abstract. Under Iqbal and Twombly, such formulaic pleading
does not cross the line from conceivable to plausible, and the claim must be
dismissed.

The fraud count fails independently under Rule 9(b), which requires that the
circumstances constituting fraud be stated with particularity. The Complaint
does not specify who made the allegedly false statements, when or where they
were made, or how they were communicated to Plaintiff, and it pleads scienter
only by labeling the conduct "knowing" and "intentional." Group pleading against
all Defendants collectively cannot satisfy Rule 9(b)'s particularity
requirement. Because the deficiencies are ones of substance rather than form,
and Plaintiff has already amended once, the Court should dismiss the fraud claim
with prejudice.
